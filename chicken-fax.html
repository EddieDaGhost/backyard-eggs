Discover fascinating facts about our feathered friends and the amazing eggs they produce!

  • Impressive Egg Production: Hens produce approximately 250-300 eggs per year, depending on the breed. That's almost one egg per day!
  • World's Heaviest Egg: An ostrich laid the world's heaviest egg at over 2.5 lbs - that's equivalent to about 24 chicken eggs!
  • Shell Color Myth: Egg shell color is breed-dependent and has no impact on nutritional value. Brown eggs and white eggs are equally nutritious!
  • Freshness Float Test: Water flotation indicates egg age. Fresh eggs sink while older ones float due to the air cell inside expanding over time.
  • Thick Whites Mean Fresh: Egg white thickness correlates with freshness. Fresh eggs have thick, firm whites, while older eggs have thinner, more watery whites.
  • Impressive Memory: Chickens can recognize up to 100 faces, including humans! They remember who treats them well.
  • Chickens Dream: Chickens experience REM sleep and can dream, just like humans do. Wonder what they dream about?
  • Diet Affects Yolk Color: Diet quality affects yolk color and richness. Chickens with access to diverse greens and insects produce deeper orange yolks.
  • Record-Breaking Hen: One hen holds the record for laying 371 eggs in a single year. Talk about dedication!
  • T-Rex Connection: Genetic research reveals chickens share closer DNA with Tyrannosaurus rex than modern reptiles do. Your backyard dinosaurs!
