    Egg Care & Handling

    Storage Recommendations

    Fresh eggs can be stored in two ways:

    • Refrigerated: Store eggs in the refrigerator for maximum shelf life (up to 4-5 weeks)
    • Room Temperature: Unwashed eggs with intact bloom can be kept at room temperature for 2-3 weeks

    Freshness Test

    Water Displacement Test: Fill a bowl with water and gently place an egg in it.

    • Sinks and lays flat: Very fresh (0-2 weeks old)
    • Sinks but stands upright: Still good (2-3 weeks old)
    • Floats: Past prime - best not to eat

    Cleaning Protocol

    If you need to clean your eggs:

    • Use warm water (warmer than the egg to prevent bacteria from being drawn inside)
    • Gently wipe with a cloth or soft brush
    • Avoid removing the natural "bloom" coating when possible - it helps preserve freshness
    • Refrigerate eggs after washing

    Food Safety
